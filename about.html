Built by builders, for builders

BaseWorker started as a simple question: where do Base builders actually go to find each other? This is our answer.

Our story

BaseWorker began in 2024 as a small Telegram group of builders shipping on Base who kept running into the same problem — great ideas going nowhere because the right person never saw them, and bounties scattered across a dozen Discord servers.

We built the first version of BaseWorker as an internal tool to keep our own group organized. Once other builders asked to use it, we knew it needed to be a real community hub — open to anyone building on Base, from solo developers to funded protocols.

Today, BaseWorker hosts thousands of ideas, hundreds of bounties, and a growing directory of live projects, all built and maintained by the community that uses it.

What we stand for

Open by default

Every idea, bounty, and project on BaseWorker is public. No invite-only circles, no gatekeeping — if you're building on Base, this is your platform too.

Builders come first

We design every feature around what helps someone ship faster — clear bounty terms, fast discovery, and direct lines between people who want to work together.

Ship in public

Progress, not perfection. We'd rather see a rough project marked "Building" than no project at all — momentum is the whole point.
