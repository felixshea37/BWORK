How BaseWorker Works

Four simple steps take you from an idea in your head to a project the whole Base community can see, fund, and use.

1

Post

Share your idea, post a bounty, or add your project in minutes. No gatekeeping — every post is public the moment you submit it. Pick a category so the right people find it faster.

2

Connect

Builders and supporters discover your post through search, filters, and the community feed. Upvotes surface the strongest ideas; applicants show up directly on bounties.

3

Collaborate

Work together, build, and bring your ideas to life. Use comments and direct messages to align on scope, milestones, and delivery before any money moves.

4

Launch

Launch your project and share it with the world. List it on the Projects page, update your progress as you ship, and let the community help you find your next supporters.

If you have an idea

Post it on the Ideas board, tag a category, and let the community upvote and comment. The best ideas often turn into bounties or full projects.

If you need work done

Post a bounty with a clear deliverable and a reward in USDC. Review applicants, pick a builder, and mark it complete once the work is delivered.

If you're building something

List your project on the Projects page with a status — Building, Beta, or Live — and update it as you progress. Supporters can find and follow you from day one.

Frequently asked questions

Is BaseWorker free to use?

Yes. Posting ideas, bounties, and projects is free. Bounty rewards are funded directly by the person posting the bounty, paid out in USDC on Base.

Do I need a wallet to post something?

You'll need a Base-compatible wallet to receive bounty payouts or fund a bounty, but browsing, sharing ideas, and listing projects doesn't require one.

How are bounty disputes handled?

For larger bounties, we recommend milestone-based escrow so payouts happen incrementally as work is delivered, reducing risk for both sides.

Can I edit a post after publishing?

Yes, you can edit your idea, bounty, or project listing at any time from your profile to keep details and status up to date.

What counts as a "Live" project?

A project is Live once it's usable by real people on mainnet — not just a demo or testnet build. Beta covers anything still being tested with early users.
